Norman Design Concepts

Norman Design Concepts are rooted in the understanding that design should cater to human needs, behaviors, and capabilities.. By focusing on simplicity, clarity, and consistency in design, Universal Design minimizes barriers and maximizes usability, creating experiences that are functional and welcoming for all users, not just those with specific needs. Here they are listed below.

  • Feedback
  • Mapping

  • User-Centered Thinking
  • Error Prevention and Management
  • Designing for emotional impact

Responsible and Flexible Layouts and Semantic Structure

Throughout the years we have used many strtegies to help those on different devices and with different disabliities. For example using a semantic structure and accesssible grids help keep the stadards up to date. Semantic structure plays a critical role in creating accessible websites. By using properly structured HTML elements—such as headings (h1>, h2>, etc.), lists (ul>, ol>, li>), and descriptive nav>, main>, header>, and footer> elements—designers and developers create a logical, meaningful framework for content. While doing this it is crucial to make sure that we adapt our sites for all devices.

WCAG and Web Standards: Ensuring Inclusive Digital Spaces

One of the most widely accepted sets of guidelines is the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG). These guidelines, developed by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C), offer practical recommendations to make web content more accessible, focusing on four key principles: Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, and Robust (often abbreviated as POUR).

Keyboard Naviagtion and Screen Readers

Keyboard Navigation and Screen Readers are important parts in ensuring that those with disablities can navigate throughout the page. Those with vision impairments may need to use a screen reader to tab through sections of the page. In order to do this they use a screen reader which is basically just a voiceover to tell them where they are at in the page. We must make sure our outline is all sorted out so this is accessible to them. Some software examples of this are Apple VoiceOver, NVA, and Microsoft Narrator.
